Our Story

53 years of baking, one family, five boroughs.

A Family Tradition Since 1972

Martha Kowalski opened her first bakery in Astoria, Queens with a single oven, a recipe book passed down from her grandmother in Warsaw, and a fierce belief that the best ingredients make the best pastries. Five decades later, Martha's Country Bakery is a beloved New York institution — still family-owned, still baking everything from scratch, still using the finest butter and freshest local fruit we can find.

53 Years of History

1972

The First Oven

Martha Kowalski opens a 400 sq ft bakery on 31st Ave in Astoria with $800, one oven, and her grandmother's recipes.

1981

Forest Hills Opens

Growing demand from Queens families prompts a second location in Forest Hills, managed by Martha's daughter, Elena.

1995

Bayside & Brooklyn

The third and fourth locations open within six months as the bakery becomes a true NYC institution.

2008

Catering Program Launch

Corporate clients and event planners drive Martha's to launch a full catering program for weddings, birthdays, and office events.

2018

Manhattan Arrives

The Upper West Side location opens — Martha's first Manhattan outpost, 46 years after that first Astoria oven.

2025

53 Years & Still Baking

Third-generation Kowalskis now manage daily operations. Same recipes. Same values. Five locations and counting.
